## Paper #1: Statistical Signatures of Majorana Zero Modes in Disordered Topological Superconductor Antidot Vortices

**Authors**: Zhibo Ren, Jukka I. Väyrynen  
**Affiliations**: Purdue University  
**arXiv**: [2604.11692 [cond-mat.supr-con]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11692)  
**Date**: April 14, 2026  
**Domain**: `superconductivity`, `quantum-info`  
**Why collected**: A novel spatial-statistics criterion to distinguish Majorana zero modes from trivial CdGM states in disordered vortex cores via STM

---

### Summary

This work develops a random matrix theory (RMT) framework to study how disorder affects Majorana zero modes (MZMs) and Caroli-de Gennes-Matricon (CdGM) states in an antidot-pinned vortex on a 3D topological insulator-superconductor platform. The key finding is that the variance of the MZM probability density is exactly twice that of CdGM states in the large-system limit, because the MZM wavefunction is real (GOE statistics) while CdGM states are complex (GUE statistics). This translates into an inverse participation ratio (IPR) ratio of $4/3$, providing a disorder-invariant spatial signature accessible to scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) beyond the conventional zero-bias conductance peak.

---

### Core Concepts

- **Antidot-Pinned Vortex**: A superconducting antidot geometry created by locally removing the superconductor in a TI-SC heterostructure. Unlike conventional Abrikosov vortices, the vortex position is spatially fixed and can host a single MZM at small magnetic fields.

- **Caroli-de Gennes-Matricon (CdGM) States**: Low-energy bound states generically present in vortex cores. They lack topological protection and respond to disorder in both energy and wavefunction.

- **RMT Low-Energy Hamiltonian**: The effective Hamiltonian projected onto the bound-state subspace is modeled as a $(2N+1)$-dimensional antisymmetric random matrix $H^R$:
  $$
  H = \sum_{i,j=1}^{2N+1} H^R_{i,j} \gamma_i \gamma_j
  $$
  It has one zero mode (MZM), $N$ positive-energy states, and $N$ negative-energy states.

- **Particle-Hole Symmetry Constraint**: The MZM $\phi_M$ satisfies $P\phi_M = \phi_M$ where $P = K$ (complex conjugation). This forces the MZM wavefunction to be real, obeying Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) statistics. In contrast, CdGM eigenspinors $\phi_{\text{CdGM}}$ are complex, obeying Gaussian unitary ensemble (GUE) statistics.

---

### Key Innovations

1. **Real vs Complex Wavefunction Distinction**: The only fundamental difference between MZM and CdGM wavefunctions in the disordered RMT limit is that the former is real while the latter is complex. This leads to distinct probability density distributions for squared components $w = |\phi^{(1)}|^2$:
   $$
   f_M(w) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}} \frac{\Gamma(D/2)}{\Gamma((D-1)/2)} \frac{(1-w)^{(D-3)/2}}{\sqrt{w}}
   $$
   $$
   f_{\text{CdGM}}(w) = (D-1)(1-w)^{D-2}
   $$
   where $D = 2N+1$.

2. **Variance Ratio of 2**: For the squared wavefunction components in the Majorana basis, the variances satisfy:
   $$
   \text{Var}(w_M) = \frac{2(D-1)}{D^2(D+2)} \approx \frac{2}{D^2}, \quad \text{Var}(w_{\text{CdGM}}) = \frac{D-1}{D^2(D+1)} \approx \frac{1}{D^2}
   $$
   Thus $\text{Var}(w_M)/\text{Var}(w_{\text{CdGM}}) \to 2$ as $D \to \infty$.

3. **IPR Ratio of $4/3$ in Nambu Basis**: Transforming to the Nambu basis, the total probability density $\rho(\vec{x}) = |u(\vec{x})|^2 + |v(\vec{x})|^2$ has second moments:
   $$
   \langle\rho_M^2\rangle \approx \frac{8}{D^2}, \quad \langle\rho_{\text{CdGM}}^2\rangle \approx \frac{6}{D^2}
   $$
   yielding $\langle\rho_M^2\rangle / \langle\rho_{\text{CdGM}}^2\rangle \approx 4/3$. This maps directly to the IPR measured in STM experiments.

4. **Experimental Feasibility**: The authors estimate that under optimal binary hypothesis testing, only $\sim 83$ independent spatial measurements are theoretically sufficient to distinguish the two distributions at $< 5\%$ error rate.

---

### Technical Approach

**STM Tunneling Conductance**: The differential conductance $G(\vec{x}, V) \equiv dI/dV$ is related to the local density of states (LDOS):
$$
G(\vec{x}, V) = \frac{G_0 \beta}{4\pi\hbar N_1(\mu_0)} \int_0^\infty dE \cosh^{-2}\left[\frac{(E-\mu_0-eV)\beta}{2}\right] \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{dE'}{\hbar} N_s(\vec{x}, E') \operatorname{Im}\frac{1}{E'-E+\mu_0 - i\eta}
$$
where the LDOS is $N_s(\vec{x}, E) = \sum_{E_n \geq 0} |u_n(\vec{x})|^2 \delta(E-E_n) + |v_n(\vec{x})|^2 \delta(E+E_n)$.

**Symmetrized Conductance**: To extract the total probability density, the symmetrized conductance $G_{\text{sym}}(\vec{x}, V) = G(\vec{x}, V) + G(\vec{x}, -V)$ is used. In the low-temperature limit and when tunneling resolves a single mode:
$$
\lim_{\eta \to 0} \frac{\sum_{\vec{x}} G_{\text{sym}}^2(\vec{x}, 0)}{\sum_{\vec{x}} G_{\text{sym}}^2(\vec{x}, E_n/e)} \approx \frac{\langle\rho_M^2\rangle}{\langle\rho_n^2\rangle}
$$

**Numerical Simulation**: A 2D spinless $p_x + ip_y$ superconductor on a square lattice is simulated using Kwant. Parameters: lattice constant $a = 25$ nm, $\mu = 100$ $\mu$eV, hopping $t \approx 400$ $\mu$eV, pairing $\Delta_0 = 100$ $\mu$eV. A stadium-shaped antidot ($L=60a$, $R=50a$) with on-site disorder uniformly distributed in $[-\Delta_0, \Delta_0]$ breaks spatial symmetries and ensures quantum chaos.

**Normalized IPR**: Within the stadium region $S$:
$$
I_{\text{norm}}(\rho) = \frac{\sum_{\vec{x} \in S} \rho(\vec{x})^2}{\left[\sum_{\vec{x} \in S} \rho(\vec{x})\right]^2}
$$
with predictions $N_{\text{site}} I_{\text{norm}}(\rho_M) \approx 2$ and $N_{\text{site}} I_{\text{norm}}(\rho_{\text{CdGM}}) \approx 3/2$.

---

### Main Results

**RMT Variance Scaling** (Fig. 2):
- Numerical simulations over 20,000 random matrix realizations for $D = 101$ to $601$ confirm the analytical variance scaling.
- The variance ratio $\text{Var}(w_M)/\text{Var}(w_{\text{CdGM}})$ converges to 2 as the sample size increases.

**Kwant Numerical Verification** (Fig. 3):
- Analysis of the 20 lowest positive-energy states across $10^4$ disorder realizations yields a mean ratio of scaled IPRs of $1.36 \pm 0.11$, in agreement with the theoretical prediction of $4/3$.
- The MZM density distribution for a single realization is shown in the inset.

**Probability Density Histograms** (Fig. 4):
- For a single disorder realization sampled over the whole stadium ($13{,}765$ sites), the CdGM-to-MZM variance ratio is $1.83$, approaching 2.
- For a uniform subsampled region ($459$ sites), the ratio is $1.29$.
- The histograms agree well with the theoretical PDFs in Eqs. (10) and (11).

**Spectral Resolution Requirement**:
- Poor resolution admixes MZM and CdGM contributions. The measured IPR then interpolates between the two limits and should never exceed the universal upper bound of $4/3$. Equal admixture yields a weighted average of $7/6$.

- [ ] **Beyond Single Vortex**: How do these statistics generalize to systems with multiple MZMs or vortex-vortex interactions?
- [ ] **Comparison with Other Platforms**: Can similar statistical signatures be engineered in nanowire-based MZMs or Fe-based superconductor vortices?

## Paper #2: Gate-Dependent Offset Charge Shifts and Anharmonicity in Gatemon Qubits in the Weak Tunneling Regime

**Authors**: Utkan Güngördü, Rusko Ruskov, Silas Hoffman, Kyle Serniak, Andrew J. Kerman, Charles Tahan  
**Affiliations**: Laboratory for Physical Sciences / UMD; MIT Lincoln Laboratory  
**arXiv**: [2604.24716 [cond-mat.mes-hall]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24716)  
**Date**: April 27, 2026  
**Domain**: `superconducting-qubits`, `mesoscopic-physics`  
**Why collected**: A first-principles many-body treatment of gatemon circuits predicts new charge-offset renormalizations and capacitance corrections missed by phenomenological models, with direct experimental signatures in charge dispersion and anharmonicity

---

### Summary

This work investigates the experimental implications of a recently derived gatemon Hamiltonian obtained from a microscopic many-body treatment of a superconductor-quantum-dot-superconductor (S-QD-S) junction in the weak tunneling regime. The model self-consistently quantizes the superconducting phase via a path-integral approach and predicts two effects missing from earlier phenomenological descriptions: (i) a gate-voltage-dependent renormalization of the effective junction capacitance $\delta C_\Sigma(\epsilon_g)$, and (ii) two distinct charge offsets, $\delta n_g(\epsilon_g)$ and $n_z(\epsilon_g)$, that emerge in junctions with tunneling asymmetry. The authors quantify the impact of these quantum-fluctuation-induced corrections on the qubit charge dispersion $\delta_{01}$ and anharmonicity $\alpha$, showing that the capacitance renormalization contributes to anharmonicity at a level comparable to the continuum-state energy shift $E_{\text{cont}}$. The central experimental prediction is an effective charge-offset shift $\delta n_{\text{eff}}(\epsilon_g)$ of the $n_g$-oscillation curves as a function of dot-gate voltage, which can be detected by repeating spectroscopy measurements at different $\epsilon_g$.

---

### Core Concepts

- **Gatemon Qubit**: A transmon-like superconducting qubit where the conventional S-I-S Josephson junction is replaced by a superconductor-quantum-dot-superconductor (S-QD-S) junction. A gate electrode atop the semiconductor enables *in situ* electrostatic tuning of the qubit frequency.

- **Andreev Bound States (ABSs)**: In a single-channel S-QD-S junction, two subgap ABSs emerge from the hybridization of the dot level with the superconducting leads. These states carry the Josephson current and determine the effective junction response.

- **Effective Hamiltonian** (even parity sector):
  $$
  \hat{H}_{\text{even}} = 4\tilde{E}_C (-i\partial_\phi - \tilde{n}_g - n_z \eta_z)^2 + \hat{U}_J(\phi)
  $$
  where $\tilde{E}_C = e^2/(2\tilde{C}_\Sigma)$ is the renormalized charging energy, $\tilde{n}_g = n_g + \delta n_g$ the shifted offset charge, and $n_z$ a second offset depending on the occupation within the junction.

- **Josephson Energy Matrix**: For a single-channel QD in the weak tunneling regime ($\Gamma_{L,R} \ll \Delta$), the Josephson energy takes a $2 \times 2$ matrix form encoding the two ABS branches:
  $$
  \hat{U}_J(\phi) = E_{J,\text{eff}}(T) \begin{pmatrix} \cos(\phi/2) & i\zeta \sin(\phi/2) \\ -i\zeta \sin(\phi/2) & \cos(\phi/2) \end{pmatrix} + E_{\text{cont}}(T)\mathbb{1}
  $$
  where $T(\epsilon_g)$ is the junction transparency and $\zeta$ the asymmetry parameter.

---

### Key Innovations

1. **Charge Offset Renormalization**: In asymmetric junctions ($\delta\Gamma \neq 0$), the quantum fluctuations of the SC phase induce two new charge offsets—$\delta n_g(\epsilon_g)$ modifying the conventional offset, and $n_z(\epsilon_g)$ depending on the number of particles within the junction. Both are functions of the QD gate voltage $\epsilon_g$ and were absent from all prior phenomenological models.

2. **Observable Effective Shift**: The combined effect produces an experimentally detectable shift of the charge-oscillation peaks:
   $$
   \delta n_{\text{eff}}(\epsilon_g) = \delta n_g(\epsilon_g) + n_z(\epsilon_g)
   $$
   measurable by tracking $E_{01}(n_g)$ at different dot-gate voltages $\epsilon_g$.

3. **Capacitance Renormalization**: The effective capacitance acquires a correction $\delta C_\Sigma(\epsilon_g, \Gamma_{L,R})$ from phase fluctuations. In the weak tunneling regime, its contribution to anharmonicity is *comparable* to that of the continuum energy $E_{\text{cont}}(\phi)$, meaning it cannot be neglected when interpreting anharmonicity anomalies.

4. **WKB Analysis of Branch Interplay**: For small $\epsilon_g$ and asymmetry ($\epsilon_g, |\Gamma_L - \Gamma_R| \ll \Gamma_L + \Gamma_R$), an extended WKB treatment adapted from the SC QPC literature captures the Landau-Zener transition between upper and lower ABS branches near $\phi = \pi$, explaining the non-vanishing charge dispersion even in the high-transparency limit.

---

### Technical Approach

**Path-Integral Effective Hamiltonian**: Starting from a many-body action for the S-QD-S junction shunted by a large capacitance, the slow-phase approximation ($e^2/2C_\Sigma \ll \Delta$) and weak tunneling ($\Gamma_{L,R} \ll \sqrt{\Delta^2 - \epsilon_g^2}$) allow reduction to an effective Schrödinger equation. The phase quantization is derived self-consistently, yielding the Hamiltonian above with renormalized parameters.

**Boundary Conditions**: Generalized boundary conditions for the even-parity sector are derived without assuming small hopping rates, extending previous treatments to arbitrary tunneling asymmetry.

**Numerical Spectrum**: The qubit transition energy $E_{01}(n_g, \epsilon_g)$ and anharmonicity $\alpha(n_g, \epsilon_g)$ are obtained by diagonalizing $\hat{H}_{\text{even}}$ with the exact Josephson matrix potential. Parameters representative of InAs/Al junctions are used: $\Delta \approx 200\,\mu\text{eV}$, $E_C \sim E_J$ in the weak tunneling regime.

---

### Main Results

**Charge Dispersion** (Fig. 3):
- For asymmetric junctions ($\delta\Gamma \neq 0$), the $n_g$-oscillation curves of $E_{01}$ exhibit a clear shift $\delta n_{\text{eff}}(\epsilon_g)$ as $\epsilon_g$ is varied.
- The shift is most pronounced in the weak-tunneling, moderate-asymmetry regime where the two ABS branches are well gapped.
- In the small-$\epsilon_g$ limit where transparency $T \approx 1$, the upper branch contributes via Landau-Zener mixing, preventing the charge dispersion from vanishing.

**Anharmonicity** (Fig. 5):
- The capacitance renormalization $\delta C_\Sigma(\epsilon_g)$ produces a contribution to $\alpha$ comparable to $E_{\text{cont}}(\phi)$ for $\epsilon_g \in (0.1\Delta, 0.7\Delta)$.
- This implies that interpreting observed anharmonicity anomalies solely in terms of continuum effects (as in Refs. [2, 7, 8]) is incomplete; $\delta C_\Sigma$ must be included for accurate modeling.

**WKB Non-Zero Intercept** (Fig. 6):
- In the high-transparency limit ($T \approx 1$), an extended WKB analysis predicts a non-zero anharmonicity intercept $\alpha(\epsilon_g = 0)$, arising from the finite gap between ABS branches.
- This provides a qualitative explanation for recent experimental observations that deviate from simple transmon expectations.

- [ ] **Asymmetry Dependence**: How do $\delta n_g$ and $n_z$ scale with the tunneling asymmetry $\delta\Gamma/\Gamma$? Is there a regime where one dominates over the other?
- [ ] **Experimental Protocol**: Design a detailed pulse sequence for detecting $\delta n_{\text{eff}}(\epsilon_g)$ via repeated $E_{01}$ spectroscopy at different $\epsilon_g$. What are the requirements on gate-voltage stability and coherence time?
- [ ] **Strong Tunneling Extension**: The model is restricted to weak tunneling ($\Gamma \ll \Delta$). How would intermediate and strong coupling modify the charge offsets and capacitance renormalization?
