## Paper #1: $\mathbb{Z}_{2q}$ parafermionic hinge states in a three-dimensional array of coupled nanowires

**Authors**: Sarthak Girdhar, Viktoriia Pinchenkova, Even Thingstad  
**arXiv**: [2604.07313](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07313)  
**Key words**: topology, superconductivity, majorana, parafermion, hinge modes  
**Year**: 2026

### Summary

A model of 3D helical second-order topological superconductor formed by weakly coupled Rashba nanowires. The system hosts gapless helical $\mathbb{Z}_{2q}$ parafermionic modes along 1D hinges, with Majorana hinge modes in the noninteracting limit ($q=1$).

### Core Concepts

- **Second-Order Topological Superconductor**: Gapped bulk and surface, but gapless hinge states
- **Rashba Nanowires**: Spin-orbit coupled nanowires with helical properties
- **Parafermions**: Generalization of Majorana fermions with $\mathbb{Z}_{2q}$ exchange statistics
- **Helical Hinge Modes**: Counter-propagating modes protected by symmetry

### Key Innovations

1. **3D Helical SOTSC**: First construction using coupled Rashba nanowire array
2. **Parafermionic Hinge Modes**: Gapless $\mathbb{Z}_{2q}$ parafermions along closed hinge paths
3. **Tunable Trajectory**: Hinge mode path controlled by interwire coupling hierarchy
4. **Majorana Limit**: Recovers Majorana hinge modes when $q=1$ (noninteracting)

### Technical Approach

- Array of weakly coupled Rashba nanowires with superconducting pairing
- Bulk and surface gap analysis for parameter regime identification
- Hinge state characterization using effective edge theory
- Symmetry protection analysis for parafermionic modes

## Paper #2: Programmable higher-order nonequilibrium topological phases on a superconducting quantum processor

**Authors**: Haoran Qian, Ming Gong, Jiahui Zhang, Shaojun Guo, et al. (Pan Jianwei's group)  
**Journal**: Science (2025)  
**DOI**: [10.1126/science.adp6802](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adp6802)  
**Key words**: topology, higher-order-topological-phase, Floquet, superconducting-qubit, quantum-simulation, chiral-density  
**Year**: 2025

### Summary

This study demonstrates both equilibrium and nonequilibrium higher-order topological phases (HOTPs) using a 66-qubit Zuchongzhi 2.0 superconducting quantum processor. The authors implemented $>50$ cycles of Floquet operators on a $6 \times 6$ qubit array and introduced a universal detection method based on chiral density dynamics to identify Floquet corner topological invariants and $\pi$-energy topological corner modes.

### Core Concepts

- **Higher-Order Topological Phases (HOTPs)**: Topological phases where boundary states emerge at $(d-2)$ or lower dimensions, e.g., corner modes in 2D systems
- **Floquet Topological Phases**: Nonequilibrium topological phases in periodically driven systems, characterized by quasienergy spectrum with $2\pi$ periodicity
- **Second-Order Topological Phases (SOTPs)**: Gapped bulk and edges, but gapless corner states
- **Chiral Density**: A measurable quantity used to detect topological invariants via dynamics of the time-evolution operator
- **$\pi$-Energy Corner Modes**: Topological corner modes appearing at quasienergy $\pi$ in Floquet systems
- **Floquet Topological Mirror Winding Numbers ($Z_0$, $Z_\pi$)**: Invariants characterizing nonequilibrium SOTPs based on the Floquet operator

### Key Innovations

1. **First Experimental Realization of 2D Nonequilibrium HOTPs**: Using a programmable superconducting quantum processor with Floquet circuits
2. **Universal Chiral Density Detection Method**: A novel approach to extract Floquet corner topological invariants by measuring chiral density dynamics
3. **$>50$ Floquet Operator Cycles**: Demonstrating long-time coherent dynamics of topological states on a 36-qubit array
4. **Direct Measurement of Topological Invariants**: Extracting $\operatorname{tr}(U^n)$ from chiral density to characterize Floquet topological phases

### Technical Approach

- **Device**: 66-qubit Zuchongzhi 2.0 superconducting quantum processor ($6 \times 6$ subset used)
- **System**: Bose-Hubbard model with tunable qubit frequencies and couplings
- **Floquet Circuit Design**: Two-layer quantum circuits implementing intracell and intercell couplings
- **Detection Protocol**: 
  - Measure chiral density dynamics $\langle\Gamma(t/2)\rangle$
  - Extract trace of Floquet operator $\operatorname{tr}(U^n)$ via Fourier transformation
  - Compute local density of states (LDOS) and Floquet corner topological invariants
- **Phase Characterization**: Mapping out topological phase diagram using Floquet winding numbers ($Z_0$, $Z_\pi$)

## Paper #3: Proximate quantum spin liquids and Majorana continua in magnetically ordered Kitaev magnets

**Authors**: Peng Rao, Roderich Moessner, Johannes Knolle  
**arXiv**: [2604.03099](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03099)  
**Key words**: Kitaev-model, quantum-spin-liquid, Majorana-fermions, inelastic-neutron-scattering, Majorana-continua, $\alpha$-RuCl$_3$  
**Year**: 2026

### Summary

This study investigates spin excitation spectra in magnetically ordered phases proximate to the Kitaev quantum spin liquid (KQSL) using a Stoner-like theory with Majorana partons. The authors compute inelastic neutron scattering (INS) intensities in the random phase approximation (RPA) and find that high-energy multi-spinon continua persist across the Brillouin zone, providing a mechanism for magnon decay distinct from conventional multi-magnon scenarios.

### Core Concepts

- **Proximate Quantum Spin Liquid**: Magnetically ordered phases near a QSL phase where confinement length remains long and parton descriptions apply to high-energy processes
- **Majorana Parton Theory**: Representation of spin operators using Majorana fermions, enabling exact solution of the Kitaev model and approximate treatment of perturbed models
- **Stoner-like Theory**: Mean-field approach treating magnetic order as condensation of paramagnon modes (bound states of partons)
- **Random Phase Approximation (RPA)**: Method to compute dynamical spin susceptibility beyond mean-field by including particle-hole excitations
- **Multi-Spinon Continua**: High-energy excitations involving multiple fractionalized spinons, distinct from single-magnon or multi-magnon excitations
- **Order-by-Disorder (ObD)**: Selection of ground state from classically degenerate manifold by quantum/thermal fluctuations

### Key Innovations

1. **Majorana RPA for Ordered Phases**: Extending Majorana parton theory to symmetry-broken phases with nonzero mean-field magnetization
2. **Multi-Spinon Decay Mechanism**: Identifying broad high-energy continua as multi-spinon excitations causing magnon damping distinct from conventional scenarios
3. **Application to $\alpha$-RuCl$_3$**: Modeling candidate Kitaev material with realistic Hamiltonian ($K$-$J$-$$\Gamma$$-$$\Gamma'$$-J_3$ model), recovering zigzag order and field anisotropy
4. **Benchmarking Against Heisenberg Model**: Demonstrating that Majorana theory correctly reproduces Goldstone modes even far from QSL regime

### Technical Approach

- **Model**: Extended Kitaev model (Kitaev-$J$-$$\Gamma$ model) on honeycomb lattice:
  $$
  H_{KJ\Gamma} = K \sum_{\langle i,j \rangle \in \alpha} S_i^\alpha S_j^\alpha + J \sum_{\text{N.N.}} \mathbf{S}_i \cdot \mathbf{S}_j + \Gamma \sum_{\langle i,j \rangle \in \alpha} S_i^\beta S_j^\gamma$$
- **Method**: Majorana mean-field theory (MFT) with self-consistent RPA for dynamical spin structure factor
- **INS Intensity**: Computed from imaginary part of dynamical spin susceptibility
- **Material Parameters**: Kitaev candidate $\alpha$-RuCl$_3$ modeled with additional $J_3$ and $$\Gamma'$$ terms under magnetic field

### Main Results

- Majorana RPA correctly captures linear Goldstone dispersion in AFM Heisenberg limit despite being far from KQSL regime
- In Kitaev-Heisenberg model, Majorana theory agrees with numerics on ground state and spin gap, outperforming linear spin wave theory
- Large INS intensities appear at high-symmetry Brillouin zone points associated with magnetic orders
- At intermediate/high energies, broad multi-spinon continua emerge across BZ as distinct decay mechanism
- For $\alpha$-RuCl$_3$: zigzag ground state and field anisotropy qualitatively match experiments; broad scattering continua recovered

## Paper #4: Majorana-assisted nonlocal spin correlation in quasi-one-dimensional Kitaev spin liquids

**Authors**: Yuki Yamazaki, Shingo Kobayashi, Akira Furusaki  
**arXiv**: [2603.25647](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25647)  
**Key words**: Kitaev-honeycomb-model, Majorana-zero-modes, nonlocal-correlation, quantum-spin-liquid, topological-order  
**Year**: 2026

### Summary

This work proposes Majorana-assisted nonlocal spin correlation as a signature of Majorana nonlocality in quasi-one-dimensional (1D) Kitaev spin liquids. In the flux-free sector of a quasi-1D Kitaev honeycomb model, Majorana zero modes localized at both ends are stabilized by finite-size-induced topology. The nonlocal Majorana fermion parity operator $P_{\text{MF}} = i\gamma_L \gamma_R$ maps to a nonlocal spin-string operator, producing end-to-end spin correlation without local magnetization.

### Core Concepts

- **Quasi-1D Kitaev Model**: Honeycomb model with finite width in one direction, creating effectively 1D geometry
- **Flux-Free Sector**: Ground state sector with zero flux ($W_p = +1$ for all plaquettes), hosting itinerant Majorana fermions
- **Majorana Zero Modes (MZMs)**: Zero-energy bound states at system boundaries with non-Abelian statistics
- **Finite-Size-Induced Topology**: Topological phases emerging due to finite-size effects and hybridization of edge states
- **Nonlocal Majorana Parity**: Operator $i\gamma_L \gamma_R$ measuring fermion parity of boundary Majorana modes
- **Spin-String Operator**: Nonlocal spin operator mapped from Majorana parity via Jordan-Wigner transformation

### Key Innovations

1. **Finite-Size-Induced Topology in Quasi-1D**: Identifying two types of MZMs—one from 2D flat-band edge states, another from finite-size-induced gap
2. **Majorana-Spin Mapping**: Showing $P_{\text{MF}} = i\gamma_L \gamma_R$ maps to nonlocal spin-string operator
3. **Nonlocal Spin Correlation**: Demonstrating end-to-end spin correlation proportional to $P_{\text{MF}} \times P_{\text{total}}$ without local magnetization
4. **Physical Signature**: Providing concrete experimental signature of intrinsic Majorana nonlocality in QSLs distinct from topological superconductors

### Technical Approach

- **Model**: Kitaev honeycomb model on quasi-1D geometry (strip with finite width)
  $$
  H = -\sum_{\langle ij \rangle \in x} J_x S_i^x S_j^x - \sum_{\langle ij \rangle \in y} J_y S_i^y S_j^y - \sum_{\langle ij \rangle \in z} J_z S_i^z S_j^z$$
- **Representation**: Jordan-Wigner transformation mapping spins to Majorana fermions
- **Flux-Free Sector**: Restriction to ground state with all $W_p = +1$ ($\mathbb{Z}_2$ fluxes)
- **Topological Invariant**: 1D winding number protecting boundary MZMs
- **Numerical Methods**: Exact diagonalization and DMRG for finite systems

### Main Results

- Quasi-1D Kitaev model hosts MZMs at both ends stabilized by finite-size-induced topology
- Two distinct MZM types: (1) connected to 2D flat-band edge states, (2) from finite-size gap
- Nonlocal spin correlation emerges between edge spins in flux-free sector
- Spin correlation is size-independent and survives without local magnetization
- Numerical calculations confirm finite nonlocal correlation from MZMs

## Paper #5: Quasi-1D Planar Magnetic Topological Heterostructure

**Authors**: Z.Z. Alisultanov  
**arXiv**: [2604.01823](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01823)  
**Key words**: topological-insulator, SSH-model, chiral-symmetry, class-AIII, winding-number, Möbius-band, Klein-bottle  
**Year**: 2026

### Summary

This work introduces a quasi-1D magnetic heterostructure of alternating 2D topological insulator (TI) and normal insulator (NI) strips. The low-energy physics is governed by a hybrid Hamiltonian combining the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) and Shockley models with spin-momentum locking and local Zeeman splitting. The system belongs to symmetry class AIII with $\mathbb{Z}$ topological invariant, supporting phases with winding numbers $\nu = 0, 1, 2$. A magnetic defect serves as a local probe with distinct in-gap spectra for different topological phases. Multilayer geometry reveals Möbius band topology with Brillouin zone compactifying into a Klein bottle.

### Core Concepts

- **Quasi-1D Topological Heterostructure**: Alternating strips of 2D TI and NI creating effectively 1D system with hybridized edge states
- **SSH-Shockley Hybrid Model**: Hamiltonian combining dimerized hopping (SSH) and interface spin-flip scattering (Shockley)
- **Symmetry Class AIII**: Chiral symmetry present ($C = \tau_z \sigma_y$), but time-reversal and particle-hole symmetries broken
- **$\mathbb{Z}$ Topological Invariant**: One-dimensional winding number computed from block-off-diagonal Hamiltonian structure
- **Winding Numbers**: $\nu = 0$ (trivial), $\nu = 1$, $\nu = 2$ (topological phases)
- **Möbius Band Topology**: Multilayer geometry with nonsymmorphic projective symmetry leading to twisted band structure
- **Klein Bottle Compactification**: Brillouin zone topology where periodicities are identified with a twist

### Key Innovations

1. **Hybrid SSH-Shockley Model**: Combining two paradigmatic models for 1D topology with spin-momentum locking
2. **Complete Phase Diagram**: Mapping all topological phases ($\nu = 0, 1, 2$) via winding number calculation
3. **Magnetic Defect Spectroscopy**: Using single magnetic impurity as local probe with distinct in-gap spectra for each phase
4. **Möbius Topology in Multilayers**: Nonsymmorphic symmetry leading to Klein bottle Brillouin zone topology
5. **Experimental Platform**: Connection to 1D moiré systems (graphene nanoribbons, twisted WTe$_2$)

### Technical Approach

- **Model**: Tight-binding Hamiltonian with spin ($\sigma$) and edge ($\tau$) degrees of freedom:
  $$
  H = \sum_n \Psi_n^\dagger (v_F k_y \tau_z \sigma_z + \Delta_F \tau_0 \sigma_x + \Delta_S \tau_x \sigma_0) \Psi_n + \sum_n \left[ \Psi_n^\dagger \Delta_z \tau_0 \sigma_z \Psi_n + (\Delta_D \Psi_{n+1}^\dagger \tau_+ \sigma_0 \Psi_n + \text{h.c.}) \right]
  $$
- **Parameters**: $\Delta_S$ (TI tunneling), $\Delta_D$ (NI tunneling), $\Delta_F$ (spin-flip), $\Delta_z$ (Zeeman)
- **Bloch Hamiltonian**:
  $$
  H(k) = v_F k_y \tau_z \sigma_z + \Delta_F \tau_0 \sigma_x + \Delta_z \tau_0 \sigma_z + (\Delta_S + \Delta_D \cos k_x) \tau_x \sigma_0 + \Delta_D \sin k_x \tau_y \sigma_0
  $$
- **Topological Invariant**: Winding number from block-off-diagonal structure
- **Symmetry Analysis**: Altland-Zirnbauer class AIII (chiral symmetry only)

### Main Results

- Phase diagram with winding numbers $\nu = 0, 1, 2$ identified via Hamiltonian block structure
- Magnetic defect in topological phase generates four dispersive in-gap states with protected level crossing
- Magnetic defect in trivial phase generates only two states, one pinned to band edge
- Multilayer geometry exhibits nonsymmorphic projective symmetry
- Möbius band topology emerges with Klein bottle Brillouin zone compactification
- Connection to realizable platforms: graphene/hBN nanoribbons, twisted 1T'$-$WTe$_2$, strained MoSe$_2-$WSe$_2$
