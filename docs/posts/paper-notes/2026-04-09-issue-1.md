## Paper #1: Josephson Current and Noise at a Superconductor-Quantum Spin Hall Insulator-Superconductor Junction

**Authors**: Liang Fu, C.L. Kane  
**arXiv**: [0804.4469](https://arxiv.org/abs/0804.4469)  
**Key words**: quantum spin hall, majorana, josephson, superconductivity, topology, proximity  
**Year**: 2008

### Summary

Seminal work exploring the interplay between topological quantum spin Hall insulators and superconductivity. The paper theoretically investigates Josephson junctions formed by superconductors on either side of a 2D topological insulator.

### Core Concepts

- **Quantum Spin Hall Insulator**: 2D topological phase with helical edge states
- **Proximity Effect**: Inducing superconductivity in the edge states via coupling to s-wave superconductors
- **Majorana Fermions**: Predicted emergence of Majorana bound states at the junction
- **Fractional Josephson Effect**: 4π-periodic Josephson current as signature of Majorana modes

### Key Innovations

1. **Theoretical Framework**: Systematic analysis of superconductor-topological insulator-superconductor (S-TI-S) junctions
2. **Majorana Signature**: Prediction that zero-energy Majorana bound states manifest as fractional AC Josephson effect
3. **Noise Characteristics**: Calculation of noise spectrum to distinguish Majorana modes from conventional Andreev bound states
4. **Experimental Predictions**: Concrete signatures for experimental detection of Majorana fermions in solid-state systems

### Technical Approach

- Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) Hamiltonian for the junction
- Green's function methods for calculating current and noise
- Low-energy effective theory for the edge states

## Paper #2: FerBo: A superconducting circuit with Andreev relaxation protection

**Authors**: J. J. Caceres, D. Sanz Marco, J. Ortuzar, P. P. Ortiz, S. Dusuel, I. Safi, E. S. Timm, J. E. Mooij, C. Urbina, A. L. Yeyati, M. April, A. Levy Yeyati, D. Estève, M. Devoret, H. Pothier  
**arXiv**: [2604.01145](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01145)  
**Key words**: superconducting qubit, Andreev levels, josephson junction, dephasing protection, relaxation protection  
**Year**: 2026

### Summary

A novel superconducting quantum circuit (FerBo) combining a large inductance, small capacitor, and well-transmitting Josephson weak link. Protection against relaxation arises from hybridization between fermionic Andreev levels and bosonic electromagnetic mode of the LC circuit.

### Core Concepts

- **Andreev Levels**: Bound states in Josephson weak link with fermionic character
- **Fermion-Boson Hybridization**: Coupling between Andreev levels and LC circuit mode
- **Relaxation Protection**: Suppressed decay due to hybridized nature of states
- **Dephasing Protection**: Phase-space delocalization as in fluxonium qubit

### Key Innovations

1. **FerBo Architecture**: Parallel arrangement of L, C, and Josephson weak link
2. **Andreev Protection**: First proposal for relaxation protection via Andreev level hybridization
3. **Wide Parameter Range**: Robustness over experimentally accessible parameters
4. **Dual Protection**: Both relaxation and dephasing protection in single circuit

### Technical Approach

- Circuit quantization with parallel L-C-Josephson elements
- Analysis of Andreev level spectrum in transparent weak link
- Numerical simulation of qubit coherence properties
- Comparison with fluxonium and other protected qubits
